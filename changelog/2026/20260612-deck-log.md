## Fixed (WebTransport multi-session stability)

- The picoquic WebTransport server survives four or more concurrent sessions and
  completes full rounds, on `feat/module-http3`. Reliable traffic rides one
  persistent framed stream per session instead of a fresh stream per message,
  which exhausts stream credit and starves the connect handshake
  ([decision](../../decisions/20260612-webtransport-persistent-framed-stream.md)).
  Five supporting fixes hold the network thread up under load: a mutex on the
  inbound queue, an inclusive wake-loop that drains and services receive on every
  callback, `get_packet_peer` peeking the queue front per the MultiplayerPeer
  contract, a connection table sized for the fanout, and the picoquic textlog
  gated behind the verbose flag.
- The queue race and the wake-loop starvation each carry a Lean+Plausible proof
  in the new [http3-queue](https://github.com/v-sekai-multiplayer-fabric/http3-queue)
  workspace: the locked queue stays size-honest for every schedule, the inclusive
  loop stays starvation-free, and the models exhibit the schedules the unlocked
  and exclusive variants fail.

## Added (the loop on both transports and OpenXR)

- The loop chooses its transport at launch through `TRANSPORT`: ENet for the
  local slice, WebTransport for the browser and headset paths, with the text
  protocol unchanged across both. Control rides reliable on channel 0, the
  high-rate transform rides unreliable on channel 1.
- An OpenXR client joins the four-player loop over LAN as a real player: it autoconnects
  to the desktop server, fights in immersive OpenXR above the frame floor, and
  the round commits progression. The headset controllers drive the loop through
  polled, edge-detected actions (left thumbstick locomotion, right trigger
  attack, right A grab, left Y teleport vote), reading the bool action `trigger_click`
  rather than a float trigger, with the XR camera held by reference so the
  locomotion branch runs.
- The [merge](https://github.com/v-sekai-multiplayer-fabric/merge) assembler
  returns to the base branch and drops the local assembly branch on every run,
  including dry runs and failures, so a half-built assembly leaves the checkout
  clean.
